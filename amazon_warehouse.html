Amazon Warehouse Discount Finder

A tool designed for Martin Lewis to help users find Amazon products at reduced prices

Setting the scene

The narrative: Hidden within Amazon's vast marketplace are incredible deals on customer returns and slightly damaged products, often sold at a fraction of their original prices. However, finding these bargains can be a challenge. To make this easier, I created a tool for Martin Lewis' 'MoneySavingExpert' website, designed specifically to unearth these hidden gems for savvy shoppers.

When: 2021

My responsibilities:

  • Wireframing / Prototyping
  • UI Design
  • Usability Testing

Business goal: Increasing sales commissions by having MoneySavingExpert users adopting the Amazon Warehouse Discount Finder.

Success metrics: On launch day alone, the Amazon Warehouse Discount Finder attracted 240k visitors. This impressive traffic translated into an estimated £600,000 in Amazon sales, resulting in approximately £36,000 in commissions.

From humble beginnings

How I ended up working on this project

One of the first projects I tackled after joining the MoneySuperMarket Group, was the Amazon Warehouse Discount Finder.

This project had been initiated previously but was shelved due to shifting priorities. When it landed on my desk, I inherited an early wireframe draft from a previous designer.

Picking up where they left off, I saw the potential to turn this paused initiative into a valuable tool for our users.

Coming up with an intuitive UI

Home Page + Results Page

Without reinventing the wheel, I leveraged established e-commerce patterns to shape the tool.

The best deals were prominently displayed on the home page, with clear details about each product's condition and price reduction.

To help users dive deeper, I included a search bar, allowing them to find specific products and refine their search on the results page.


Usability Testing

What do our end users think of it?

Our Manchester office has a user testing facility which I set up for the occasion.

Methodology: Face-to-Face usability testing with 10 users





User testing results

Main task: Find a good deal on an electronic item of your choice.

For most participants, the tool's purpose was immediately clear. Nearly all navigated the process of buying a product with only minor issues.

Every participant expressed excitement about using the tool in the future.
